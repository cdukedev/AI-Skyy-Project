What started as a small outreach center for students seeking English-language and citizenship studies, the Hialeah Campus continues to grow to meet the educational needs of the community. In 1980, the campus moved to its permanent location in Hialeah. Since then, the campus has expanded its program offerings and services to meet the growing educational needs of the community, and in 2005, Hialeah became Miami Dade College’s seventh campus. In 2015, the campus doubled in size with the addition of a new building to serve a growing student population. Hialeah campus has an art gallery and state-of-the-art laboratories equipped for studies in chemistry, biology, physics, and technology.

Associate in Science degrees
